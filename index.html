LoonReader

An eReader with open hardware, software, and content

Source Code

An Experiment


I write software. I design what users see, how to deliver it, how to change it.

Hardware is something different. Hardware is hard.

We need devices that tell stories, or even become part of a story. Not another tablet off of an assembly line.

LoonReader starts off as an Arduino with a touchscreen cover.

LoonReader's software and content will update from the internet, receiving new stories and changing how the device works.

GPS for location-based, sun-averse, and moon books


The Flora GPS receives GPS signals to determine LoonReader's coordinates. That means you can have stories which change based on your location. Or guides which take you from one place to another.

It's also possible to calculate time, position of the sun, and phase of the moon. So you can have a werewolf story whose ending isn't revealed until the full moon, or a story told by different animals during the day and night.
